1. Treatment of Severe Malaria and Its Associated Complications

Severe malaria remains the number one killer in our community. At Dza Kwelin Children's Hospital, we approach diagnosis and treatment with urgency and precision.

Our rapid diagnostic tests and microscopic blood smears confirm the parasite presence and density. Intravenous artesunate is our first-line treatment, delivered swiftly to stop the infection in its tracks.

Severe malaria often leads to life-threatening anemia, which is why we prioritize timely, safe blood transfusions. Our team ensures rigorous blood typing, cross-matching, and careful monitoring to improve patient outcomes and save lives.

2. Treatment of Various Pediatric Infections

From meningitis to skin infections and abscesses, our hospital uses a comprehensive strategy to treat a wide array of childhood infections.

Lumbar punctures and CSF analysis enable fast meningitis diagnosis. URTIs like pneumonia and TB are managed with targeted antibiotics and supportive care.

We treat UTIs, salmonellosis, and other bacterial infections with evidence-based therapy. Skin infections like impetigo and cellulitis are treated based on severity with topical or systemic medications.

3. Emergency Management of Common Pediatric Ailments

The Dza Kwelin Children's Hospital is equipped to handle a range of common pediatric emergencies with prompt and efficient care.

For severe respiratory distress, immediate interventions include oxygen therapy, nebulization, and, if necessary, intubation and ventilation. Febrile seizures are managed with antipyretics and anticonvulsants, with thorough investigations to identify the underlying cause.

In cases of severe dehydration due to vomiting or diarrhea, rapid fluid resuscitation via IV lines or oral rehydration therapy is prioritized. 4. Pediatric Neurosurgical Care

The Dza Kwelin Children's Hospital, with its dedicated pediatric neurosurgery unit, routinely performs critical procedures. These include VP shunt placement for hydrocephalus, surgical closure of neural tube defects like myelomeningocele, and emergency burr hole drainage for intracranial hematomas.

The unit also manages complex skull fractures requiring surgical repair and undertakes procedures for brain and spinal cord tumors. With our volunteer neurosurgeons, this specialized unit ensures timely and expert neurosurgical care for children with diverse neurological conditions.

5. Community Education and Public Health Impact

At the Dza Kwelin Children's Hospital, community education is a cornerstone of our mission. We've reached over 10,000 households and 60,000 women, significantly reducing craniospinal congenital malformations through pre-conceptual folic acid awareness over five years.

Additionally, over 30,000 Mogode teenagers have been educated on HIV and Hepatitis B/C prevention and treatment, fostering a proactive approach to health within the community.

6. Treatment of Severe Malnutrition and Its Associated Complications

Dza Kwelin Children's Hospital addresses severe malnutrition with a phased approach tailored to resource constraints.

Initial stabilization focuses on treating dehydration with oral or intravenous rehydration solutions and managing infections with affordable antibiotics. Nutritional rehabilitation begins with F-75 therapeutic milk, transitioning to F-100 as tolerated. Micronutrient supplementation, deworming, and psychosocial support are integrated.

Emphasis is placed on educating caregivers for sustainable home-based care and prevention.
